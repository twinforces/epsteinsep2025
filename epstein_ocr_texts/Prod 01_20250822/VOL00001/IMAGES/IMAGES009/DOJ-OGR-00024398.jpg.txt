3 plead guilty to scheme to smuggle contraband into prison
The Washington Times
int/
The U.S. Attorney's Office in Maryland says three people have pleaded guilty in connection with a scheme to
smuggle contraband into a state prison. A news release says the three entered their pleas on Tuesday to a
federal racketeering charge. Their plea agreements say they conspired with inmates and others to smuggle
narcotics, cell phones, unauthorized flash drives, tobacco and other contraband into the Maryland
Correctional Institute at Jessup.
